# Research

## When to use this skill

Use when a task requires gathering information, comparing options, or understanding a landscape before making decisions.

## Process

1. Frame the research question precisely — what do we need to know and why?
2. Identify 3-5 high-quality sources (official docs, peer-reviewed, authoritative)
3. Extract key findings and organize by theme
4. Note contradictions between sources
5. Identify gaps — what we still don't know
6. Rate confidence level for each finding (high/medium/low)

## Source quality hierarchy

1. Official documentation and primary sources
2. Peer-reviewed research and industry reports
3. Expert blog posts and conference talks
4. Community discussions and forums
5. AI-generated summaries (lowest confidence — verify independently)

## What to avoid

- Do not present speculation as fact
- Do not rely on a single source for any critical finding
- Do not skip gap identification — knowing what you don't know is valuable
